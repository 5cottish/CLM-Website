New Reading Club Launched in Kira Sub-county

We've opened our 5th community reading club, reaching 50 new children with literacy support and educational resources in one of Wakiso's fastest-growing communities.

On a bright Monday morning in Kira Sub-county, the sound of children singing filled the air as Christian Legacy Makers launched its newest community reading club. This marks our fifth such club in Wakiso District and represents a significant expansion of our literacy programs.

Why Kira Sub-county?

Kira is one of the fastest-growing urban areas in Wakiso District, with a rapidly expanding population of young families. However, access to quality educational resources remains limited, particularly for children from low-income households.

"We identified Kira as a priority area because of the high number of out-of-school children and the lack of safe spaces for learning," explains our Program Director. "This reading club will serve as both an educational hub and a safe haven for vulnerable children."

The Launch Day

The launch event brought together over 100 community members, including parents, local leaders, and the 50 children who will participate in the program. The day included:

  • Opening prayers and dedication by local church leaders
  • Distribution of reading materials and school supplies
  • Interactive storytelling sessions
  • Registration of children into the program
  • Training for volunteer reading facilitators
"I am so happy that my children will have a place to learn and grow. This reading club is an answer to our prayers." — Grace Nakato, Parent of Three

Program Structure

The Kira Reading Club will operate three times per week, offering:

  • Guided Reading: Structured sessions using age-appropriate materials
  • Phonics Training: Foundational literacy skills for beginners
  • Book Lending: Take-home books to encourage family reading
  • Homework Support: Assistance with school assignments
  • Creative Activities: Drawing, storytelling, and drama

Community Partnership

The success of this initiative relies on strong community partnerships. We are grateful to collaborate with:

  • Kira Sub-county Local Council
  • St. John's Anglican Church, Kira
  • Local primary schools
  • Parent Teacher Associations

Looking Ahead

This reading club is part of our broader goal to establish 10 community reading clubs in Wakiso District by the end of 2026. With your support, we can reach even more children with the gift of literacy.

"Every child deserves the chance to read, learn, and dream," says our Executive Director. "Today, 50 more children in Kira have that chance."
